Recovery Pie

The whole pie reflects the total number of confirmed cases of people infected by coronavirus in South Korea.

Daily Flow

The height of a single bar is the total number of people suffered from Coronavirus in South Korea and confirmed to be infected. It includes three parts: those who could or could not recover and those who are currently in the active phase of the disease.

Daily Speed

This graph shows the speed of growth (in %) over time in South Korea. The main three parameters are the number of confirmed cases, the number of recoveries and failures. The orange line is the speed of changing of the number of active cases (i.e., of those, who are still ill).

Note 1. In calculations, a moving 3-day average values it used.

Note 2. When the speed is positive, the number of cases grows every day. The line going down means that the speed decreeses, and while there may be more cases the next day, the disease spread is slowing down. If the speed goes below zero, that means that less cases registered today than yesterday.
